<p><p>Glass Ionomer Cement (GIC) for cementations is categorized into three main types: powder, liquid, and set. The powder component contains glass particles that react with an acid, while the liquid consists of an acid-base solution that activates the powder. The set form refers to the hardened state after the chemical reaction occurs, providing strong adhesion and thermal properties. Each type is tailored for specific dental applications, offering benefits like biocompatibility, fluoride release, and ease of use in clinical settings.</p></p>
