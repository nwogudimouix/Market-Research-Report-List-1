<p><p>The industrial grade magnesium hydroxide market can be classified based on production methods: chemical synthesis and physical methods. Chemical synthesis involves the reaction of magnesium salts with hydroxides or carbonates, yielding high-purity magnesium hydroxide suitable for various applications, including flame retardants and wastewater treatment. In contrast, physical methods typically entail the precipitation of magnesium from brine solutions or seawater through evaporation, producing a more environmentally friendly but potentially lower-purity product. Both methods cater to different industrial needs and specifications.</p></p>
