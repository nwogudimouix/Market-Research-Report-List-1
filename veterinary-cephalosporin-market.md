<p><p>The Veterinary Cephalosporin market is categorized into four generations, each offering distinct antimicrobial properties. First-generation cephalosporins primarily target Gram-positive bacteria and are commonly used for skin infections. Second-generation variants provide broader Gram-negative coverage, useful in respiratory infections. Third-generation cephalosporins are more potent against resistant bacterial strains, making them suitable for serious infections. Fourth-generation cephalosporins offer enhanced activity against both Gram-positive and Gram-negative bacteria, appealing for critical care in veterinary medicine, addressing complex infections effectively.</p></p>
