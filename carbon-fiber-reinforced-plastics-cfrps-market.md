<p><p>Carbon Fiber Reinforced Plastics (CFRPs) are advanced composite materials combining carbon fibers with plastic resin, offering exceptional strength-to-weight ratios and durability. The market is primarily segmented into two types: thermosetting CFRPs and thermoplastic CFRPs. </p><p>Thermosetting CFRPs are characterized by their permanent, inflexible structure once cured, making them ideal for applications requiring high strength and thermal resistance. In contrast, thermoplastic CFRPs offer greater flexibility and ease of processing, allowing for recycling and reshaping, making them suitable for mass production and varied applications.</p></p>
