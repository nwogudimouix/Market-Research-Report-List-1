<p><p>Electromagnetic field (EMF) measuring devices are categorized into two main types: single-axis and tri-axis. Single-axis devices measure electromagnetic fields along one direction, making them useful for specific applications where orientation is known. In contrast, tri-axis devices measure fields simultaneously in three orthogonal directions, providing a comprehensive assessment of the EMF environment. Tri-axis devices are typically preferred for more accurate and holistic evaluations, accommodating varied field orientations and offering greater insight into EMF exposure levels in diverse settings.</p></p>
