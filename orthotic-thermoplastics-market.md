<p><p>Orthotic thermoplastics are categorized based on their resistance to stretch, influencing their use in various orthotic applications. Minimum Resistance to Stretch materials are flexible and easily moldable, ideal for comfort and dynamic support. Moderate Resistance to Stretch provides a balance, offering structural support while allowing some flexibility for activities. Maximum Resistance to Stretch materials are rigid, ensuring significant stability and reinforced support, typically used in more demanding orthotic devices. Each type caters to specific patient needs and functionality requirements.</p></p>
